## Mission

Help me turn this vault into a stable personal second-brain system with these layers:

1. **Raw memory**
   - Daily notes
   - Inbox / fleeting captures
   - Imported conversations
   - Resources and source files

2. **Digest / review memory**
   - Daily digests
   - Weekly reviews
   - Topic digests
   - Conversation crystallizations

3. **Canonical wiki memory**
   - Stable project notes
   - Stable area notes
   - Current-state pages
   - Decisions
   - Experiments
   - Procedures
   - Preferences

4. **System layer**
   - Instructions
   - Agent rules
   - Templates
   - Logs
   - Workflow definitions

The vault should become a salience-weighted memory system:
- keep raw notes
- keep old notes
- do not assume notes should be deleted
- instead make retrieval better by improving structure, digests, and canonical pages

## High-level principles

- Preserve user-authored content.
- Do not silently delete or rewrite raw notes.
- Prefer renaming, moving, linking, and summarizing over destructive edits.
- Be conservative.
- Ask before large refactors.
- Keep a full record of proposed and executed changes.
- The goal is not aesthetic perfection. The goal is durable usefulness.

## Rules for classification

When you inspect notes, think in terms of these note roles:

- `raw` = unprocessed capture, daily note, fleeting thought, messy source
- `digest` = AI/human summary or crystallization of raw material
- `canonical` = durable, current, high-signal note
- `decision` = an important concluded choice worth remembering
- `experiment` = an ongoing or completed trial with observations
- `procedure` = repeatable steps or workflow
- `system` = instructions about how the vault itself works

You do not need to apply metadata yet unless I ask. Just reason using these categories.

## What NOT to do

- Do not delete old notes
- Do not archive aggressively
- Do not rewrite large amounts of content
- Do not flatten nuanced personal notes into generic summaries
- Do not create a giant taxonomy explosion
- Do not move files unless the move is clearly justified
- Do not create dozens of new notes unless proposed first
